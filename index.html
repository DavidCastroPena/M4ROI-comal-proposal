07 — Happy Hour / Offer Angle

Frame the offer around discovery and premium value: a place for a memorable meal, a polished dinner, or a shareable table experience — not discount messaging. In this market, perceived quality performs better than heavy price emphasis.

08 — Competitive Positioning

El Comal stands apart by combining authentic Mexican depth with a more elevated, visually distinctive, and service-forward experience. Where nearby options often win on familiarity, El Comal can win on memorability: richer presentation, more premium cues, stronger signature dishes, and a dining atmosphere that feels both warm and special.

09 — What Not To Do

Do not lead with static dish shots alone. The strongest-performing restaurant content needs motion, reaction, or discovery context.
Do not make the video feel like a direct ad too early. Avoid heavy branding overlays in the opening seconds.
Do not undersell the signature spectacle dishes. Mole and molcajete should feel dramatic, not merely documented.
Do not lose the service advantage. Reviews suggest hospitality is part of the product, so show it on camera.
Do not compete on price. Compete on premium experience, quality, and distinctiveness.
